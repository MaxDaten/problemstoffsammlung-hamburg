# Impeccable — Project Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Hamburg residents of all ages trying to answer one question fast: **"When and where can
I drop off my hazardous waste near me?"** Context of use is mixed — often on a phone,
sometimes outdoors or standing in a kitchen deciding whether today is a collection day;
sometimes on desktop while planning. Audience spans tech-comfortable and not, including
older users and non-native German speakers (hence DE / EN / Deutsch Leichte Sprache).
This is an **unofficial** civic visualization of the Stadtreinigung Hamburg schedule.

Primary jobs to be done:
- Find the next collection date for a specific street/district.
- Find the nearest collection point and its upcoming dates.
- See all 2026 dates for a location and add them to a personal calendar.

### Brand Personality
**Trustworthy & civic** — calm, clear, dependable; it should read like a well-made public
notice, not a startup dashboard. Three words: **dependable, plain-spoken, Hanseatic**.
Emotional goal: quiet confidence and relief ("I found my date, I trust it"). Never anxious,
never playful-for-its-own-sake. Authority comes from clarity, not decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction
An **editorial** evolution of the current Stadtreinigung-red civic look — keep the civic
identity, but raise the craft well above generic civic/dashboard sites.

- **Typography (replaces the current system font):** Display/headlines in **Spectral**
  (refined editorial serif, screen-optimized) for masthead, section headings, and large
  dates — it carries the "authoritative public notice" tone. UI/body/data in **Public Sans**
  (built for government services) for the list, popups, controls, and small print —
  maximal legibility at small sizes. Strong scale contrast (≥1.25 ratio), few sizes.
- **Theme:** **Auto (follow system)** — design a *genuine* dark variant for evening use,
  not an inversion; the OSM map and red accent must stay legible in both. Default the data
  display to whatever the device prefers.
- **Color:** Keep **Stadtreinigung red (~#e2001a)** as a precise, rare accent (60-30-10:
  mostly tinted neutral surfaces, red only for emphasis/identity). Move palette to **OKLCH**;
  tint neutrals subtly toward the brand hue; no pure #000/#fff. Status colors (soon / later /
  done) must **not rely on color alone**.
- **Layout:** Left-aligned, asymmetric composition with real spatial rhythm (4pt scale,
  `gap`-based, varied not uniform). The map is the stage; the sidebar/list is the editorial
  column. Avoid card-on-card and uniform card grids.
- **Anti-references:** generic admin/dashboard templates; cyan-on-dark / purple gradient
  "AI" look; glassmorphism; gradient text; colored left-border accent stripes; system-font
  blandness. (These are also hard bans in the Impeccable guidelines.)

### Accessibility
**WCAG 2.1 AA** baseline (appropriate for a public-facing civic service; aligns with German
BITV expectations). Specifics that matter here:
- Status of a marker/location (next ≤14 days / later in 2026 / finished) must be conveyed by
  **shape or icon plus color**, not red↔green alone (red-green color blindness).
- Full keyboard operability with visible focus (do not suppress focus outlines on controls).
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; respect `prefers-color-scheme`.
- Maintain legibility of small data text (dates, slots) — this is why the body face is a
  government-grade sans.

### Design Principles
1. **Find-my-date first.** Legibility and speed of the core task outrank every aesthetic
   choice. If a flourish slows comprehension, cut it.
2. **Editorial, not decorative.** Character comes from a confident type pairing
   (Spectral × Public Sans), real hierarchy, and spatial rhythm — never from ornament,
   gradients, or glass.
3. **Civic palette, used with restraint.** Stadtreinigung red as a rare accent over tinted
   OKLCH neutrals; status never color-only.
4. **Two honest themes.** Light and a purpose-built dark variant, derived from system
   preference, both keeping map + accent legible.
5. **Accessible and multilingual by construction.** AA, keyboard-first, reduced-motion aware;
   coherent across DE / EN / Leichte Sprache (Leichte Sprache favors spelled-out, simple
   presentation — e.g. full weekday/month names, no dense abbreviations).
